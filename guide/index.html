EGFF 2024 · Esri GIS Fundamentals Foundation
Pass the EGFF 2024 in three days flat.

You can pass this exam with 10.5 focused hours. The Esri GIS Fundamentals Foundation 2024 is rated easy-to-moderate for anyone with GIS theory - but it has one critical trap: you must pass all four domains individually, not just hit an overall score. Your strong theoretical background is a major advantage since the exam tests conceptual understanding ("which tool?") rather than button-click memorization.

30 concepts most likely on your exam
Data Discovery - 35% - memorize these first
  • Vector vs. raster data - points/lines/polygons vs. grid cells; when to use each
  • GCS vs. PCS - angular (lat/lon) vs. linear (meters/feet); datum (WGS84, NAD83); ellipsoid
  • Decimal degrees vs. DMS vs. UTM - coordinate unit formats
  • Feature class vs. shapefile vs. geodatabase - Esri's data storage hierarchy
  • Living Atlas - Esri's curated global data; how to discover and add it
  • Web GIS - benefits, architecture, cloud-based access
  • Adding data - from geodatabases, CSV/Excel (with X/Y), portals, geocoding
  • Explore tool, bookmarks, map navigation in ArcGIS Pro
Mapping & Visualization - 25%
  • Symbology types - single symbol, unique values, graduated colors, graduated symbols, proportional symbols, heat maps, dot density
  • Smart Mapping - ArcGIS Online's automatic symbology suggestions
  • Labeling - how to generate labels, label placement properties
  • Basemaps - how to change them in Pro and Online
  • Layer properties - visibility range, transparency, drawing order in Contents pane
  • Map scale - large vs. small, reference scale
Processing & Analysis - 22%
  • Buffer, Clip, Dissolve, Intersect - know what each does conceptually
  • Select by Attributes vs. Select by Location - query types
  • Attribute tables - viewing, sorting, selecting, field calculations, joins
  • Editing in ArcGIS Pro - feature templates, Edit tab, creating/modifying features
  • Geoprocessing - what it is, where tools live in ArcGIS Pro
Content Management & Sharing - 18% - do NOT skip this
  • Sharing levels - Private, Group, Organization, Everyone (Public)
  • Validate map before sharing from ArcGIS Pro - the analyze step
  • ArcGIS Online organization structure - members, roles, groups, items, folders
  • Web map vs. web layer - what each is, when to create each
  • Content page - how to organize items, create groups, manage content
  • Pop-ups - configuration in ArcGIS Online

Exam day strategy - 72 seconds per question
01
First pass - 60–65 min
Answer confidently or eliminate two wrong answers, make your best guess, flag the question, move on. Never more than 2 minutes per question. At 30 min → ~question 25. At 60 min → ~question 50.
02
Second pass - 15–20 min
Return to flagged questions with fresh eyes. Later questions sometimes provide context clues for earlier ones. Re-read the question stem - look for keywords you missed (NOT, EXCEPT, BEST, MOST).
03
Final pass - 5–10 min
Verify no questions are left blank - unanswered questions count as wrong. Don't change answers unless you have a specific reason. First instincts based on solid knowledge are usually correct.
04
Elimination rules
Answers with absolutes ("always", "never", "only") are usually wrong. If two options are opposites, one is likely correct. The longest, most specific answer is often right. Factually true but doesn't answer what was asked? It's a distractor.
⚡
Quick tips - don't forget anything
🕐
You need exactly 10.5 hours
1.5h Monday · 7h Tuesday · 2h Wednesday morning. Protect those hours. Skip social media entirely on Tuesday.
🎯
Four domains, all mandatory
You MUST pass all four domains individually. A 100% score on three domains CANNOT save a failed fourth domain. This is the #1 way people fail.
📋
EIG is your bible
Every skill listed in the Exam Information Guide is a potential question. If you can't explain a skill confidently, study it. Go through it line by line.
🔗
Content Management kills veterans
The smallest domain (18%) is the most dangerous. Study sharing levels, groups, organizations, and the validate-before-sharing workflow until you know them cold.
🧠
Active recall beats reading
After every study block, close the material and explain it aloud from memory. This "blurting" method doubles retention compared to passive re-reading.
🚫
Don't waste time on out-of-scope topics
Python, ModelBuilder, StoryMaps, Field Maps, Survey123, 3D data, GPS, raster processing - none of these are on the exam. Zero minutes on them.
✓ Read the EIG and create your personal gap map (confident / shaky / don't know) before studying anything else
✓ Complete all four domain blocks on Tuesday - don't skip or shorten the Sharing block, even if you're tired
✓ Use the FormaSIG practice test as a learning tool, not a score tracker - understand every wrong answer
✓ Write your cheat sheet by hand on Tuesday evening - the act of handwriting reinforces memory
✓ On exam morning: eat protein + complex carbs, moderate caffeine, stop studying 20 minutes before the exam
✓ If online proctored: wired internet, cleared desk, webcam tested, Pearson VUE account verified the day before
✓ During the exam: flag uncertain questions and move on immediately - never spend more than 2 minutes on any single question
✓ Leave zero questions blank - an unanswered question is a guaranteed wrong answer
⚠ The one thing that could trip you up
  • ArcGIS Online organizational workflows - if you don't regularly share content or manage groups at work, Content Management is your weak link. You must pass it separately.
  • Esri-specific terminology - you may know the theory but not the exact Esri term. The EIG and Quizlet set cover all the vocabulary you need.
  • Assuming your work experience covers everything - the exam tests specific Esri workflows that may differ from how you do things at work. Validate against the EIG.
  • Skipping the practice test - even one full practice run reveals which domain's questions you actually struggle with, not just which content you think you know.
